Flipkart Business Case

Product Similarity Linking

üìå Business Case

Flipkart wants to group similar products (e.g., same model listed by different sellers) together so users get a clean experience with comparison options.

üß† Algorithms Used

  • TF-IDF / Cosine Similarity: Compare titles, specs, and descriptions.
  • MinHash + LSH: Efficient near-duplicate detection across millions of listings.
  • Disjoint Set Union (Union-Find): Merge linked similar items.

üß† Intuition

Just like grouping similar photos, Flipkart deduplicates catalog clutter to show customers the most relevant, clean results for one product model.

üí• Impact

Improves buyer trust, increases conversions by enabling quick comparison of seller prices and delivery times.

üíª Sample Base Code (Cosine Similarity - Python)

from sklearn.feature_extraction.text import TfidfVectorizer
from sklearn.metrics.pairwise import cosine_similarity

products = [
  "Redmi Note 12 6GB RAM 128GB",
  "Redmi Note 12 Smartphone - 6GB 128GB",
  "Samsung Galaxy M14 Blue 4GB 64GB",
  "Redmi Note 12 Pro 8GB 128GB"
]

vectorizer = TfidfVectorizer()
tfidf_matrix = vectorizer.fit_transform(products)
similarity = cosine_similarity(tfidf_matrix)

for i in range(len(products)):
  for j in range(i+1, len(products)):
    print(f"Similarity({i},{j}):", round(similarity[i][j], 2))
